My Favorite Tank


An M4 Sherman on patrol in France

Details

Name: M4 Sherman (Variants named from M4A1 - M4A6)

Units Built: 49,234

Armor Thickness: 63.5 - 93.1 mm (M4A1)

Armament: 75 mm gun (M4); 76 mm gun (M4A1 - M4A6), 2x .50 caliber Browning machine guns

Service History: Served in the US Army from 1942 to 1957

Description

The M4 Sherman is my favorite tank because of its effectiveness in both the infantry support and anti-tank role. While other tanks of the time such as Germany's Panzer III or Britain's Cromwell focused either on supporting advancing troops or destroying enemy tanks, the M4 Sherman proved to be a versatile design that allowed it to do both tasks at the same time. The design was also cost-effective and cheap, allowing for nearly 50,000 Shermans to be produced. However, even though the M4 Sherman could perform both roles, it could not excel in either of them. A good description would be that it was the tank of all trades, but the master of none; a trait it shared with its Russian counterpart, the T-34. Whatever problems faced the Sherman, it is undoutebly one of the best tanks of WWII, and has been used in many conflicts by other countries in the years since.
