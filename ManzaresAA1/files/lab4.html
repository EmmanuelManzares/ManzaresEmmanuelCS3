My Favorite Novels

"1984" George Orwell Dystopian
"Fahrenheit 451" Ray Bradbury Dystopian
"Foundation" Isaac Asimov Science Fiction

Descriptions

"1984" by George Orwell

The novel "1984" is set in that year, in an alternate world where there are three nations fighting for supremacy and influence worldwide. The focus of the novel being on "Oceania", a totalitarian regime set to control the minds of all its citizens and to swiftly put down any form of dissent or unrest. However, one man named "Winston Smith" finds himself questioning the government's actions and policies, eventually getting caught and tortured to the point where he is forced to give up any form of rebellion.

"Fahrenheit 451" by Ray Bradbury

Much like "1984", "Fahrenheit 451" takes place in a dystopian setting - the main character, "Guy Montag" is a "fireman", however unlike a normal fireman, his job is not to put out fires, instead he and his colleagues are tasked to burn books as a way to repress individuality and self-expression through writing. As time passes by, he starts to question the morality and the justification for the mass censorhip taking place.

"Foundation" by Isaac Asimov

Isaac Asimov's "Foundation" is set in a futuristic galactic society where a new branch of mathematics has been discovered. This new kind of math is named "psychohistory", which is able to predict events thousands of years into the future. Using this, a mathematician by the name of "Hari Seldon" predicts the collapse of the Galactic Empire, after which there is a period of 30,000 of anarchy and violence across the galaxy before a Second Galactic Empire is established and order is restored.
